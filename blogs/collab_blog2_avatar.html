The Digital Avatar


Brief: Design a way for people to embody and enact the outer appearance of a digital avatar.
Crew: Effy, Mu Lin, Zhihan
Duration: 1 Week


Violet Disruption: The reference video for this brief



This brief sounded the most confusing and abstract one amongst all the MA UX briefs. Our constraint was we include both physical and digital elements in the design, by involving the character of Violet, the digital avatar, who has been through sexual violence. From brain-storming different ideas to explore, we chose expressions of emotions such as frustration, tension, fear, and anger as a way to be liberated.

We started off body storming by covering our heads and acting out ways to express our frustration, tension, anger, etc etc. The action of stamping and banging the floor, crushing clothes and shouting were found repetitive in all of our actions. We picked the same elements to be reflected in our design as a way of liberation for Violet.



Pictures and videos from body storming


While ideating, I mentioned how we could show liberation digitally while physically expressing ourselves. Effy suggested there could be something digitally growing out on the ground as we stamp and express rage. We loved how our avatar was translated on the floor as we physically moved over it. For the physical element, we designed 4 suits that help express each action like hitting, crushing, squeezing and tearing. Bubble wrap material was chosen as the base for all of them as it metaphorically signifies protection for the person as they might hurt themselves by expressing emotions that involve hitting, smashing, banging etc. For the digital part, with the help of my techie boyfriend, we had used generative art in P5j to get the sound input from actions and produce the colorful flowy patterns as the output. We agreed to stage a performance where we express the emotions wearing costumes and later ask volunteers to try the same.

Rough sketch of the staging idea

Trying on the costumes


While discussing our ideas with the tutors, we realized a few loopholes like, asking a volunteer to feel the rage just by saying it, whether the costumes would help or restrict a person’s expressions, deciding how to engage people without forcing things and whether the digital graphics can be be simplified as a result of a heavy emotions. We knew we had a lot of things but struggled for quite some time to put them all in the right places. As we kept simplifying the concept, we landed on creating an impactful performance where I enact the emotional journey of Violet on a large white cloth that dramatically grows into a colorful flowy land digitally as the movements get intense, hence the energy is translated into a free flowing element of liberation. We worked on a simple yet elegant bubble wrap dress to be worn by me and be self destroyed while expressing frustration/rage/anger etc.

Using the common space near the staircase, we projected the digital avatar from the first floor to the ground floor as I performed on a white sheet. The play lasted for about 2 minutes with a poetic introduction spoken aloud by Effy. The overall experience created a massive impact for the viewers as they could sympathize with the character. I was extremely joyous as this piece combined theater with design, as I always seek such an opportunity. The constructive feedback was to have strong yet simple digital feedback for each physical action that created an immediate impact rather than the flowy nature of patterns.

My Learnings:

  • Having constraints in the brief helped to be directional yet explore the domain independently. We constantly pushed ourselves to bring fresh digital elements.
  • I realized how creating an embodied experience doesn’t always have to include the user's body in the design but letting them connect emotionally, mentally, etc can also be an embodied experience.
