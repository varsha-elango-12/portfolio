Considering body movements and languages, I came across two important subjects that dealt with body movements and their importance.

  1. The Feldenkrais method by Moshe Feldenkrais is a somatic education that uses movements and directed attention to help people increase their flexibility, coordination, efficiency, and gracefulness in their bodily movements (About, 2022).

  2. The Laban Movement Analysis by Rudolf Laban is a method and language of describing, interpreting, visualizing, and documenting human movement, especially helping dancers, actors, athletes, and musicians (‘Laban movement analysis’, 2022).

Inspiration from Art form

Having glanced over various Indian art forms, the chhau dance caught my interest. Especially the Seraikkella Chhau, a dance drama, all performers wear a single kind of mask, hiding all their facial identities and hence communicating the story by using the rest of the body primarily (Mayurbhanj Chhau, no date). Passing on the art forms over generations, the style of the dance form has become more abstract.

Communication of what?

To narrow down the scope of the initial brief, I chose to study the communication of emotions through the body excluding the face as its the most prominent feature widely used to convey emotions directly.



Redefined the Brief: Exploring communication of emotions through the human body without using face, taking inspiration from theater and art forms.

Research Question: How does the body help in emoting emotions for a human?


Emoting Workshop:

I conducted an individual workshop with people to express emotion by providing prompts to only use certain parts of their bodies and by hiding their faces. With who: 10 students at LCC, from different ethnicity, between the age of 19-28.

Aim: To explore how people express themselves with their bodies, by doing, but with limited resources of the body. How is it perceived then?

The prompts are:

  • Express happiness with your whole body
  • Express anger just with your hands
  • Express sadness just with your chest’
  • Express fear just with your abdomen/ stomach
  • Express surprise with your body, by tying your hands back
  • Express disgust/ hate just with your legs


Images of participants acting out emotions.


Highlighted analysis from the workshop:

  • Almost all of them enacted the emotions without actually feeling the emotion. Most of it was recalled from past events/experiences.
  • Most of them were observed moving their hands unconsciously while expressing their actions.
  • The actions involve sounds made by the participants with their voices, by interacting with the floor, and space around them.
  • Based on the feedback from the participants, the repeated response was how difficult it felt to express themselves with limited resources. They were not aware of how certain parts of the body are changed in expressing emotion, as such emotions are unconsciously expressed in any situation.

How is it perceived by others?

The recorded actions of the participants were shared with random other people to guess what was being conveyed in each snippet. Based on the collected feedback,

  • Not everyone succeeded in guessing the original actions but it was repeatedly noticed that happiness, anger, and sadness were rightly guessed in most of the responses.
  • Every action had some guesses made by the perceivers, holding a new story. There was no action that didn’t have any expression attached to it.
  • On the whole, even without a facial expression, emotion is still being conveyed amongst people.

Scope to improvement:

  • To have associated sounds, pictures, and objects to enhance participants to feel the actual emotions to enhance expressing them.
  • To have given head gears, and let the participants move around in space, rather than staying still behind the curtain.
