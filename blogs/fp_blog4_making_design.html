Making Design


For the final outcome, I wanted to create an explorative space that invites people to realize their interoceptive senses, by masking their outward projections, through movements, accompanied by music.

What: A space to discover our hidden movements by focusing on our bodies.

Concept: Can I focus more on my inward self by masking my outward projections?

Intended Aim: Trying to feel the unconscious interoceptive senses in our body

  • by freeing the mind and body by disconnecting from our own external self
  • by freeing the mind and body of all identities we hold in society


Storyboard of the stages of ‘Our Hidden Movements’, a spatial experience:

  1. Receiving the participants and comforting them

  2. Bringing into the experiential space

    The darkness allows them to concentrate as I briefly talk to them to focus on their body for the next 15 minutes and do whatever their body feels like doing. I prompt with examples of interoceptive senses like ‘When was the last time you listened to your heartbeats or felt butterflies inside your stomach’ to remind them about what kind of things they can possibly listen to from their body while focusing.

  3. Introducing the ‘faceless face mask’

    After talking through the brief, the mask is provided as a ‘superpower’, as it's a metaphor to suffice disconnection from oneself. It’s an external power given for time being to help focus on themselves, internally.

  4. The experience begins

    The participant is all alone, accompanied by the music, free to explore their body and mind in this space. The ring light defines the safe space while the limited ambient warm lights provide a fearless, comfortable mood for the user.

  5. Experiencing the space alone

    The participant is left alone for 15 minutes to do whatever they feel like doing, while it’s all being video recorded under their consent, for future analysis. Expecting that the 15 minutes of time can allow the first few minutes of warming up or seeping through, and then slowly letting themselves feel free in the environment.

  6. Watching their own hidden movements

    The viewing allows the participant to directly see their external state of being, as they underwent a new experience of understanding and focusing on their bodies. It can spark multiple emotions as we see ourselves in such a state, which will be analyzed later.

  7. Feedback time

    All the little experiences provided for the participant are discussed to elaborate on how they felt doing what they did. It was voice recorded for analysis later.


Designing the details

  1. Faceless face mask:

    • The form of the mask is kept organic over the face, to ensure comfortable breathing. There’s velcro attached to two ends, which allows adjusting for different users with varying head sizes
    • A black color mask creates less visibility for the participant to avoid unnecessary distraction yet can slightly see the outside, confident to move.

    The faceless face mask

  2. Music, the companion:

    What: Instrumental music of the Tabla, a percussion instrument, having Indian origin, is heavily used in Indian traditional music forms (‘Tabla’, 2022).

    Why: The music from tabla, produces a vibration that enhances synchronism in the mind, body, and other senses of the body. The beats can motivate the user to move their body (adminpar, 2019).

    Chosen Music - Song: Alla | Album: Tala Matrix | Group: Tabla Beat Science | By: Zakir Hussain and Bill Laswell

  3. Safe space design:

    Initial considerations for space: To provide a tactile and free sense to move.

    Chosen space:

    • An indoor, almost familiar space, to make the participant feel safe, enclosed, protected, and free.
    • Not completely dark to ensure fearlessness in their mind, hence focusing on other emotions in the space.
    • It was finally a teaching room at LCC, with carpeted flooring, turned into the space for exploration, as it was the closest option available at the provided time.
    • Light ring: The safe space inside the room was defined by a ring of light on the floor.

    Time of Experience:

    • 15 minutes inside the space, alone
    • This is so because the first 5 minutes or so allow the user to seep through and get comfortable in the space. Only then, they can slowly feel less conscious of being present in the space, to let loose.

  4. Who - Participants:

    I picked a random 9 people between the ageS of 18-35, from different ethnicities, studying at LCC, studying elsewhere, and strangers who work to have diversity in analysis. Seven of them were completely unknown to me, while two were my friends to see how unfamiliarity and familiarity affect the experience of being free.
