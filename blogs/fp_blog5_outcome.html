Conclusions

  1. Time is an essential factor

    • 8/9 participants mentioned the influence of time that got them comfortable, unconscious, and free to move over the period.
    • Inspired by Feldenkrais’s proposal, ‘ Body, Environment, and mind are indivisible ones, constantly changing with time’

  2. Music as a facilitator/ inspiration of movements

    • As intended, the music drove the participants to move, and also facilitated their movements majorly.
    • Different types of tapping is seen across almost all the participants as the music involves a drum/ tabla beat constantly.
    • Participants altered their speed according to the rhythm of the music. While some moved fast for faster beats, some relaxed by lying or sitting on the floor for the same beats.
    • It also caused some internal changes or provoked the interoceptive senses within a few participants. Feeling calming, relaxing, tiring, focused, prolonged, meditative, energetic, peaceful, lost in thoughts, unconscious, sleepy, and therapeutic are some of the words described by the participants which fall into the interoceptive senses of our bodies.

  3. Evidence connecting external body movements and internal body:

    • All the people who moved (7/9) pointed out certain simple and complex discoveries of their interoceptive senses in the feedback.
    • Not everyone was able to focus on their body the whole time, few got bored in between and engaged with elements in the environment.
    • Everybody except one sat on the floor. Some even lay down.
    • There was lots of stretching and exercising observed.
    • Most of them moved in circles or rotations. Did the ring light as a safe space influence such a type of movement?


Overall Conclusions

  • ‘Our Hidden movements’ experiment provoked and brought out some expected and unexpected connections between movements and the internal body of a person.
  • By altering the environment, a human did go through a new version of mind and body, in a time span of 15 minutes, which triggered certain hidden movements and realizations from their body.

Unexpected outcome

  • In the process of combining the hidden movement of all the participants, there came out a performance of movements, by non-practitioners.
  • It serves as the hidden capabilities of our bodies, in a space where we stay close to our own internal bodies, by masking our outward projections.
  • While the participant has his/her own story to their movements, each viewer will now see their own story out of it.

Scope to improvement in the experience design:

  1. While 3/ 9 felt the light in the room, comforting and safe, the rest suspected that they could have been even more comfortable and carefree, in a much darker environment. Although I agreed and hoped for a darker room, due to the video recording requirement, it demands a minimum light source in the room, as I didn't want any adulterations in the video of the person. In the future, this technique can be further explored in a much darker room, with sufficient technical support.
  2. Although the sides of the masks were fully open and ventilated, as some of them moved a lot, they felt sweaty inside the mask as time prolonged. The next version of the mask can be carefully redesigned for such an event too.


What next? Recommendations:

I believe the scope of the project exists even further when it involves science and psychology as it deals with human behavior analysis. With a somaesthetic design approach, there came out positive responses of allowing the participants to focus on their inner selves by moving, in 15 minutes time span.

  1. Can this evolve into a type of technique or method to enhance awareness of our own bodies through movements?

    Apart from self benefits, can we extract any other benefits?

  1. The performance of movements film, the other outcome, turned out to be a collaborative ‘performance art’, raising awareness about interoceptive senses and movement alterations due to society’s gaze.
  2. There’s scope for a research method to study the state of people’s alter egos through experiments in a controlled environment as such.

    3. This is not all. I would like to continue to apply the resulting analysis to various possible scopes that I come across to spark new opportunities.
