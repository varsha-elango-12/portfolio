Midpoint


Brief: Design a digital/physical marketplace for the buying and selling of human bodily fluids that prioritises trust.
Crew: Ramya, Amber, Boxun
Duration: 2 Weeks


Week 4

We were now already moving into the interim week. Considering the feedback, we narrowed it down to a situation of selling clean urine for a drug test. With various user groups like college students, police/army officials, corporate employees, etc, going through drug tests, we researched procedures and rules for different personas. As a method of ideating with real objects, we quickly picked a few objects around us which could be a potential container to exchange urine, for the different user groups.

Images from the ideating through objects experiment


Almost all objects from what we picked were stuff that's already present in the user's environment, disguised as real but with urine inside it, to avoid others doubting/ suspecting anything. We chose to focus on â€˜employee persona' as the frequency of drug testing is higher and the risk of getting caught can suspend them from work. Under employees, we narrowed down to police officials as the criticality of being caught is high, with their repetition at stake, hence trust being the utmost element to rely on. We created a fake situation to role play and understand the actions. As we roleplayed an exemplar, we realized the possible design interventions part of the system and began to design them. To understand the scenario with real locations, we visited the Southwark police station, and spent time observing the environment outside, possible places to exchange, etc.

Pictures around Southwark Police station


Elements considered in our design concept


To communicate the working concept, we shot a quick film enacting the roles and design solutions part of it and presented it during the interim presentation.


The short film conveying our idea in a possible scenario


Although people liked the film, they were unclear about our solutions in regard to building trust factor as it wasn't explained by us well enough. We lacked to put-together our research insights and ideas into the presentation, which led to confusion amongst the audience. During our feedback, all the points brought up by our tutors were already part of our concept, but not conveyed earlier. So, we were asked to focus more on conveying the concept clearly, with a more realistic film to be shot. We were also asked to work on a high-fidelity prototype of the urine container, which was part of our concept.


Week 5

While internally discussing post interim week, we realized that not documenting our process more visually than verbally amongst ourselves was the drawback of poor conveying of our concept. So, we went back to finishing all the missing pieces from our past work. It includes,

Trust journey in existing Gumtree buyer journey map

We then analyzed the highest and lowest trust points in each journey, compared them with each other, and chose the essential trust factors to consider reinforcement through our design.

The chosen factors reinforcing trust in the exchange journey.


With all these in place, clear and distinct, we now matched the design ideas we already had to address each pain point and discussed new ideas for the missing areas. While vigorously working on them, there came the Jubilee Long weekend, to put us all on a relaxed long break and get back only next week to continue the project.
