The End


Brief: Design a digital/physical marketplace for the buying and selling of human bodily fluids that prioritises trust.
Crew: Ramya, Amber, Boxun
Duration: 2 Weeks


Week 6

Moving towards the last few days of the project, we had aimed to achieve a lot. From our trust journey mapping, we narrowed down the most significant points to reinforce trust through our design and that includes,

  • How to find a suitable and trustworthy seller?
  • Is the urine clean, pure, or real?
  • Will the meeting point to exchange be safe?
  • What container can collect and store urine easily and securely? (Requirements: Sealed, concealed, easy to carry, easy to test)
  • How to know if the urine is real and clean while at exchange?
  • What kind of payment can guarantee a safe transaction that motivates exchange of urine?
To prototype our solutions for each of the addressed points above, we divided our work amongst ourselves. While Ramya was detailing and modeling the urine container, I was working on the new script to shoot a film including all our design elements in it for our final outcome. Amber and Boxun were creating the low fidelity wireframes of how it would work on the Gumtree app.

Our Design Solutions


We presented all of them except for the final film and container prototype on the week 6 presentation day. We were to present the film for the final week, Week 7 presentation. Our feedback this time was quite positive. We had improved in the way of presentation, hence conveying all our design elements clearly. Our constructive feedback was to include our ideas on how this system will come into existence as 'Apple juice () = human urine' on the Gumtree platform. Also to situate the film as real as possible.


Week 7

We started off writing down our exact tasks pending for the final presentation. It helped us stay on track and finish as per plan. As we moved ahead, we changed 'Apple Juice' to 'Yellow Juice' as there might be people who really look for apple juice and might end up buying the wrong ones. Rebranded the Gumtree's logo color to Yellow, to use it as a signifier around this concept's establishments.

Branding as Yellow Juice


Branding across recommended place for exchange


Considering the feedback from last time to enable the system to kickstart by letting people know about it, we picked digital memes as the medium to spread the word. This way, it can be unregulated and also indirect to address the niche audience.

Few memes we created


Meanwhile, the film's script had moved towards addressing fetish users this time to show how our concept includes different kinds of users. Ramya digitally modeled the container and we got it 3D-printed, it looked so cute.

The Yellow Juice container and it's size variations for various users


I had an idea to attach a plastic bag to the bottle that opens up as an outer package for it so that the buyer can carry it comfortably with no hesitation touching the container directly.

Bag for the container


The final task was shooting the film. Boxun and I decided to act again as we couldn't find new actors on short notice. We managed to finish the shoot in 4-5 hours and the rest of the time was spent on post-production and making presentations. Our film had come out really good, in terms of script, edit, and cinematography. We were quite proud of what we achieved overall.

The Yellow Juice film


We presented the same in the final presentation, having curated the ppt even more carefully to sum up all we did to the audience right. Our feedback was quite positive. They particularly liked our attention to detail in the container design, sticking the labels at exchange points, etc. The Gumtree partners liked our secured payment methods, counter-culture attitude, and overall approach. Even the camera angles in the film were quite appreciated and were encouraged to add more filters to show its under-ground characteristics. Overall, we were happy and contented.


My overall learning from this project:

  • I realised the importance of synthesizing the results out of research to put it in use. We were lacking in it, and as a result, our initial concepts were shallow. We picked it up later.
  • Maybe due to language barrier, group dynamics were lagging constantly. It was reflected in our interim presentations as well. We voluntarily rectified towards the finals.
  • Assigning works based on each of our strengths, yet constantly being flexible to jump in and refine each other's work, rendered quality to our overall output.
  • Attention to detail was something my group was keen about. I admired how we thought about the smallest details which brought in depth to the project.
